What is Evolution

Evolution is the change in characteristics in a species over time

​Charles Darwin developed the  now widely accepted theory of evolution based on his observations in the Galapagos. The theory of evolution states that all species originate from one ancestor. As this ancestor and its descendants reproduced, their genes would mutate and give them slightly different traits with different pros and cons.

Natural Selection Shown With Giraffes
Darwin proposed that evolution happened by natural selection. Natural selection (also known as survival of the fittest) is the concept that those with traits that are fit to survive in a certain environment will reproduce, while those without such traits die off. Eventually, this would result in a species that could survive in the niches of the environment it's in. As seen with the Image to the left, two of the giraffes are not tall enough to reach the leaves, and therefore can not get food and will not reproduce. The giraffe on the far left is tall enough to reach the food so it will survive and reproduce.
 Another key factor in evolution is competition. In every ecosystem on Earth there is competition, whether it is for food, shelter, etc. there is competition. This high level of competition is what requires the organisms to be fit for their environment. For example, if a certain species somehow had an infinite food supply and no predators, then that species would have a very difficult time evolving because pretty much all of them would survive and reproduce. In the picture to the right two gazelles are fighting (likely either for a food source or for a mate)  the stronger one will likely win the fight.
